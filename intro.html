A ROOM IS NOT WHERE YOU ARE

This project begins with a simple question:
what makes a place feel like home?

For a long time, I believed that home might exist somewhere else.
That perhaps, if I moved far enough,
I would finally find a place where I could fully belong.

But distance did not resolve that feeling.
In some places, I remained a stranger.
In others, I felt unexpectedly at ease.

Through this work, I began to understand
that a room is not defined by location.

It becomes something else
when I can stay without questioning myself,
when leaving is no longer the first instinct.

This is an ongoing archive of spaces I have moved through—
moments where a place,
however temporary,
felt close to something like home.
